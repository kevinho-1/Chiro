Gonstead - this is a hands on technique that involves adjusting the lower body

Latest News

News.

  • News
  • One of the highest leading reasons for a chiropractic visit has shown to be reoccurring low back pain and because of this, an article has been formed studying a randomised controlled trial assessing the effects of chiropractic manipulative therapy on patients with low back pain. 'Adding Chiropractic Manipulative Therapy to Standard Medical Care for Patients With Acute Low Back Pain' (Goertz C.M., Long C.R., Hondras M.A., Petri R., Delgado R., Lawrence D.J., Owens E.F. & Meeker W.C., 2013) studies the changes in levels of pain within patients while undergoing treatment given by chiropractors known as manipulative therapy. Patients included within the trial were mainly consisting of men and women in the military aged between 18 and 35 but the article still emphasises the benefits of spinal manipulation on patients with low back pain
  • News
  • Being a randomised controlled trial, it consisted of 91 patients who were separated into two different groups, both undergoing standard medical care (SMC), while one group being treated with chiropractic manipulative therapy (CMT) on top of their standard medical care treatment. (Goertz C.M. & et al) During week 2 of the trial, 28 and 39 patients of the SMC and SMC + CMT group respectively, were analysed for follow ups and treatment allocation. The final analysis included 29 patients from the SMC group and 40 patients from the SMC + CMT group which included evaluating a numerical scale of back pain after the 4 weeks of treatment using the back pain functional scale (BPFS) (Goertz C.M. & et al).
  • News
  • The article includes a scale where patients were reviewed on how much treatment has helped their chronic back pains. Scaling from 1 -7 where 1 indicates that the pain is completely gone, and 7 being much worse. Figure 2 shows a significant change in pain due to manipulative therapy where approximately 35% of patients rated on the scale, 2, meaning 'much better', approximately 2.5% rate 1, approximately 30% rate 3, meaning moderately better (Goetz C.M. & et al). Limitations of the controlled trial included loss of follow up, leading to disproportionate reviews between the two groups which may have been crucial to show a wider range in terms of difference in pain scale between the groups. Limitations also included not being able to blind the patients to whoever the treating clinician was (Goetz C.M. & et al).Being one of the leading causes of chiropractic visits, it is important for more evidence to be gathered about chiropractic manipulative therapy so that patients with chronic back pain may have a broader sight on which type of treatments are available to them.
